Wii U Gamepad Redesign


Introduction: The Gamepad is the primary method of input and interface on Kyoto-based Nintendo Co. Ltd.'s Wii U console. A render of the Gamepad can be found on their website. The goal of this project for my Human Factors in Design course was to identify shortcomings in an existing consumer technology, and to design a solution that addresses these pitfalls. Our team decided to focus on three key areas: optimizing the physical size of the device to improve button accessibility; improving the battery life; and overcome the challenges of a resistive touchscreen.




Identifying Needs through User Personas
To begin designing an appropriate redesigned gamepad, we had to first consider who our target audience was: children and their parents. Of course, video games are meant to have universal appeal, but the children demographic is a large market for video games, and especially Nintendo. Furthermore, this demographic requires special consideration in designing a product so that they are able to comfortably use the product.

Through brainstorming requirements for our child persona, we developed a list of specific requirements:

  • A controller that is comfortable to play on for medium to high durations of time
  • Quick television to gaming transition
  • Easy to understand indicators on the controller
  • A controller that will easily fit into small hands

However, the persona for our parent yielded different unique requirements:

  • Easy setup procedures so the device can be moved around the house at will
  • Quick and simple transition to movie and TV show playing applications
  • Less components, reducing chance for system failure and thus reducing the need to call support

The Redesign
The illustration above shows the new design and the changes made to the system from Nintendo's original controller. Some of the highlights include:

  • A safety strap added to the controller to increase safety of the device
  • Provide a clear and apparent notification of battery status
  • Portability of the device should be improved to encourage movement with the device
  • Increase controller ergonomics while shrinking its size
  • Upgrade the screen resolution so that it provides higher image quality
  • Upgrade the touch control from resistive to capacitive, to allow easier touch input and unlock additional controls through multi-touch
  • A slider design so that physical controls can be hidden when the application (e.g. movies, Internet browsing) don't require them



A Gateway to Entertainment
The slider was made possible because the control sticks were changed from analog control sticks to digital nubs, similar to those found on the Nintendo 3DS systems and Nintendo's prototype for the Wii U controller. This was a difficult decision, since analog control allows for more precise character maneuvering in a 3D space. However, this opportunity cost allowed for greater versatility of the gamepad, as it can now be used to enjoy movies, and other non-interactive entertainment. A growing use for tablets and set-top boxes is to play digital on-demand video content (e.g. Netflix, Hulu), and removing unnecessary noise from the user's peripheral vision would lead to an optimal viewing experience.
